## 1. Welcome

The GMO (Global Market Outlook) platform is a report-building tool that lets you create professional, interactive financial reports for web and print. Instead of building reports manually in PowerPoint, you use a structured editor with built-in tools that help you:

- Build charts automatically from your data files
- Recreate charts from existing images
- Generate written content using AI
- Publish reports to the web instantly
- Translate reports into French automatically
- Export to PowerPoint and PDF when needed

This guide walks you through every step, from opening the editor to sharing your finished report.

1. Open your web browser and go to **https://gmo-prototype.sanity.studio**
2. Sign in with your account credentials
3. You will see the main dashboard with a list of existing reports on the left-hand side

### What You'll See

The editor is split into two main areas:

- **Left panel** â€” A list of all reports in the system. Click any report to open it.

## 3. Creating a New Report

### Step 1: Start a New Report

1. In the left panel, click the **"+"** button or **"Create new"** to start a new report
2. Select **"Report"** as the document type

### Step 2: Fill in the Report Details

You'll see the following fields at the top of the editor:

**Report Title** (required)
- Enter the title of your report, for example: *"Global Market Outlook â€” January 2026"*
- Maximum 200 characters

**URL Slug**
- This is generated automatically from your title â€” you don't need to change it
- It creates a web-friendly version of your title for the report's web address

**Publication Date** (required)
- Select the date this report should be published
- Defaults to today's date

**Author**
- Enter the author name
- Defaults to "AXA Investment Managers"

**Status**
- Choose the current stage of your report:
  - **Draft** â€” Work in progress
  - **Review** â€” Ready for review by others
  - **Approved** â€” Approved and ready to publish
  - **Published** â€” Live and visible to readers

**Cover Image** (optional)
- Upload an image to use as the report's cover
- You can adjust the focal point of the image using the hotspot editor (see [Uploading Images](#13-uploading-images))

**Executive Summary** (optional)
- Write a brief overview of the report (2â€“3 sentences)

### Step 3: Add Your Sections

Below the report details, you'll see the **Report Sections** area. This is where you build the body of your report by adding different types of sections. Click **"Add item"** to add your first section.

> **Tip:** A typical report follows this order:
> 1. Title Section (cover)
> 2. Navigation Section (table of contents)
> 3. Header Section (section divider)
> 4. Content Sections and Chart Insights Sections (your main content)
> 5. Timeline Section (if applicable)

## 5. Adding a Title Section

The Title Section is the cover page of your report. It's typically the first section you add.

### Fields to Complete

1. **Heading** (required)
   - The main title displayed on the cover
   - Maximum 100 characters
   - Example: *"Global Market Outlook"*

2. **Subheading** (optional)
   - A supporting line below the main title
   - Example: *"January 2026 â€” Monthly Review"*

3. **Background Type**
   - Choose between:
     - **Solid Colour** â€” A flat colour background
     - **Hero Image** â€” A full-width photograph or graphic

4. **If you chose Solid Colour:**
   - Select from: Primary Green, Coast Blue, Metallic Copper, or White

5. **If you chose Hero Image:**
   - Upload a full-width image
   - The system automatically adds a dark overlay so your text remains readable

6. **Company Logo** (optional)
   - Upload a logo to display in the bottom-left corner of the cover

## 6. Adding a Navigation Section

The Navigation Section creates a table of contents for your report. It automatically lists the sections that follow it.

### Fields to Complete

1. **Navigation Title**
   - The heading shown above the table of contents
   - Default: *"In This Report"*

2. **Show Page Numbers**
   - Toggle on or off
   - When on, each item shows a number (01, 02, 03, etc.)

3. **Layout**
   - Choose how the table of contents is displayed:
     - **Vertical List** â€” A simple stacked list
     - **Horizontal Grid** â€” Items arranged in columns
     - **Cards** â€” Visual cards with optional images

4. **Card Images** (only if you chose the Cards layout)
   - For each card, you can upload an image that appears alongside the section title
   - Set the **Section Number** to match which section the image belongs to (1 for the first section after navigation, 2 for the second, and so on)
   - Images are positioned to the right side of each card

> **Tip:** The Cards layout with images creates a more visually engaging table of contents. Images bleed slightly over the right edge of each card for a modern look.

## 8. Adding a Content Section

The Content Section is the most versatile section type. It displays text content and can optionally include a chart alongside it.

### Fields to Complete

1. **Title** (required)
   - The section heading
   - Maximum 100 characters

2. **Subtitle** (optional)
   - Supporting text below the title
   - Maximum 200 characters

3. **Colour Theme**
   - Choose the background colour for this section:
     - **None** â€” White background (default)
     - **Blue**, **Green**, **Orange**, or **Brown** â€” Coloured background with white text

4. **Section Image** (optional)
   - An image or illustration that appears above the text content

5. **Content**
   - The main body text of your section
   - You can format text using:
     - **Bold** and *Italic*
     - Bullet point lists
   - Use bullet points for key insights â€” they display more clearly than long paragraphs

6. **Include Chart** (toggle)
   - Switch this on to add a chart to the section
   - When turned on, you'll see additional chart options (covered in [Working with Charts](#11-working-with-charts))

7. **Chart Source** (only when chart is included)
   - Attribution text shown below the chart
   - Example: *"Source: Bloomberg, December 2025"*

8. **Layout** (only when chart is included)
   - Choose how text and chart are arranged:
     - **Chart on Left** â€” Chart appears on the left, text on the right
     - **Chart on Right** â€” Text on the left, chart on the right
     - **Chart Full Width** â€” Chart spans the full width below the text

> **Tip:** When your section has a chart, an **AI Content Assistant** card will appear at the top of the section. This lets you automatically generate a title, subtitle, and bullet points based on your chart data. See [Using AI Content Suggestions](#12-using-ai-content-suggestions).

## 9. Adding a Chart Insights Section

The Chart Insights Section is designed for when you want to highlight key takeaways alongside a chart. It pairs a chart with a coloured "Key Insights" panel.

1. **Title** (required)

2. **Subtitle** (optional)

   - Add a chart using the Chart Builder (see [Working with Charts](#11-working-with-charts))

4. **Chart Source**
   - Attribution text shown below the chart
   - Example: *"Source: Bloomberg, 2025"*

5. **Insights Position**
   - Choose where the insights panel appears relative to the chart:
     - **Right** (default), **Left**, **Top**, or **Bottom**

6. **Insights Background Colour**
   - Choose the colour of the insights panel:
     - **Green** (default), **Blue**, **Orange**, or **Brown**
   - Text in the panel is always white

7. **Key Insights**
   - Add individual bullet points that will appear in the insights panel
   - Click **"Add item"** for each new insight
   - These are displayed as a bulleted list under the heading "Key Insights"

> **Tip:** This section also supports AI Content Suggestions. When a chart is present, you'll see the AI Content Assistant card, which can generate insights for you automatically.

## 11. Working with Charts

Charts can be added to **Content Sections** and **Chart Insights Sections**. There are two ways to create a chart: from a data file (spreadsheet) or from an existing chart image.

### Where to Find Chart Controls

When you enable charts in a section, the **Chart Configuration** area shows buttons:

- **Add Chart** â€” Create a new chart from a spreadsheet
- **Upload Image** â€” Create a chart from an existing chart image
- **Edit Chart** â€” Modify an existing chart (appears after a chart has been saved)
- **Remove Chart** â€” Delete the chart from this section

---

### 11.1 Creating a Chart from a Spreadsheet

Use this when you have data in an Excel or CSV file.

**Step 1:** Click **"Add Chart"**

A pop-up window titled **"Chart Builder"** will appear with a file upload area.

**Step 2:** Upload your file

- Drag and drop your file onto the upload area, or click to browse your computer
- Supported formats: Excel (.xlsx, .xls) or CSV (.csv)
- Maximum file size: 10 MB

**Step 3:** Wait for AI analysis

You'll see a loading message: *"Analyzing your data with Claude..."*

The system reads your data and determines the best way to display it as a chart.

**Step 4:** Review the recommendation

The system shows you:

- A **Recommended Chart** with a live preview
- A **"Why this chart?"** explanation box describing why this chart type was chosen for your data
- **Alternative Charts** â€” 2â€“3 thumbnail previews of other chart styles that could also work

**Step 5:** Choose your preferred chart

- To keep the recommendation, leave it selected
- To try an alternative, click any of the thumbnail previews at the bottom â€” it will swap in as the main chart

**Step 6:** Save

Click **"Save Chart"** to apply your selection, or **"Cancel"** to discard.

---

### 11.2 Creating a Chart from an Image

Use this when you have an existing chart (from a PowerPoint, report, or screenshot) that you'd like to recreate.

**Step 1:** Click **"Upload Image"**

A pop-up window titled **"Chart Image Import"** will appear.

**Step 2:** Upload your chart image

- Drag and drop, or click to browse
- Supported formats: PNG, JPG, JPEG, WebP
- Maximum file size: 10 MB

**Step 3:** Wait for AI analysis

You'll see: *"Analyzing chart image with AI..."*

The system examines your image, extracts all the data points, labels, and values, then recreates the chart digitally.

**Step 4:** Review and edit the extracted data

The system shows:

- A **recreated chart** based on what was found in your image
- An **Extracted Data** table below the chart

The data table is fully editable:
- **Click any cell** to correct a value if the AI misread it
- **Click any column header** to rename it â€” this automatically updates the chart legend
- First column values are left-aligned (these are your categories or dates)
- Number columns are right-aligned

**Step 5:** Choose chart style

- Review the recommended chart
- Click any **Alternative Chart** thumbnail to try a different style

**Step 6:** Save

Click **"Save Chart"** to apply, or **"Cancel"** to discard.

---

### 11.3 Editing an Existing Chart

If you've already saved a chart and want to make changes:

1. Click **"Edit Chart"** in the Chart Configuration area
2. The Chart Builder reopens with your current chart loaded
3. If the chart was created from a spreadsheet, you'll see the chart with alternatives
4. If the chart was created from an image, you'll see the chart with the editable data table
5. Make your changes and click **"Save Chart"**

### 11.4 Removing a Chart

To remove a chart from a section:

1. Click **"Remove Chart"** in the Chart Configuration area
2. The chart is removed and the buttons return to **"Add Chart"** and **"Upload Image"**

## 12. Using AI Content Suggestions

When a chart has been added to a **Content Section** or **Chart Insights Section**, the system can automatically generate written content based on your chart data.

### Where to Find It

A blue card appears at the top of the section:

> **AI Content Assistant**
> Generate title, subtitle, and content based on your chart data
>
> **[Generate Content]**

### Step-by-Step

**Step 1:** Click **"Generate Content"**

A pop-up window titled **"Generate Content with AI"** will appear. Generation starts automatically.

**Step 2:** Wait for suggestions

You'll see: *"Analyzing chart data with AI..."* followed by *"Generating content suggestions"*

**Step 3:** Review the suggestions

The system shows a card for each field it can generate. Depending on your section type, you may see suggestions for:

- **Title** â€” A section heading based on your chart data
- **Subtitle** â€” Supporting text
- **Content Bullets** â€” Key points for the body text
- **Key Insights** â€” Bullet points for the insights panel (Chart Insights Sections only)

Each suggestion card shows:
- The **current value** (what's currently in the field)
- The **suggested value** (what the AI recommends)
- A **checkbox** to include or exclude the suggestion

All suggestions are selected by default.

**Step 4:** Choose what to accept

- **Untick** any suggestions you don't want to use
- The badge at the top shows how many are selected (e.g., "3 of 4 selected")

**Step 5:** Take action

You have three options:

- **"Accept Selected"** â€” Applies the ticked suggestions to your section fields and closes the window
- **"Regenerate"** â€” Discards current suggestions and generates new ones
- **"Decline All"** â€” Closes the window without making any changes

> **Tip:** Always review AI suggestions before accepting. They're a starting point â€” you can edit the text further after accepting.

### Using the Hotspot Editor

4. Click **"Done"** or close the editor

> **Tip:** Setting a hotspot is especially important for cover images and section images, which may be cropped differently on desktop and mobile screens.

## 15. Publishing Your Report

### Understanding the Status Workflow

Reports move through four stages:

1. **Draft** â€” You're still working on it
2. **Review** â€” It's ready for others to review
3. **Approved** â€” It's been approved for publication
4. **Published** â€” It's live and visible to readers

### How to Publish

1. Set the **Status** field to **"Published"**

Once published, your report is immediately available at the live report URL. There's no delay â€” changes appear as soon as you publish.

> **Tip:** You can continue editing a published report. Your changes become visible as soon as you publish them again.

## 17. Viewing the French Translation

The platform automatically translates your report from English into French.

### From the Editor

1. Make sure your report is **published** (the translation only works for published reports)
2. In the toolbar, click **"Voir traduction francaise"** (the translate icon)
3. The French version opens in a new browser tab

If the button is greyed out, it means the report hasn't been published yet. Publish your report first, then try again.

### What Readers See

- The French report appears at **https://gmo-report.vercel.app/fr/**
- A brief loading message appears: *"Chargement de la traduction..."* (Loading translation...)
- All text is translated into formal business French
- Charts, numbers, percentages, and proper nouns remain unchanged
- An information banner at the bottom explains this is an automatic translation, with a link back to the English version

### What Gets Translated

- Report title and summary
- All section titles and subtitles
- Body text content
- Chart labels and axis labels
- Key insights
- Timeline item headings and descriptions

### What Stays the Same

- Chart data (numbers)
- Image content
- Dates and percentages
- Proper nouns and abbreviations

> **Tip:** The translation is generated fresh each time someone views the French page. After you update and re-publish content, the French version will automatically reflect your changes.

## 18. Exporting to PowerPoint

The platform can generate a branded PowerPoint presentation from your report, with optional AI-powered design review.

### Step 1: Open the Export Window

1. In the toolbar at the top of your report, click **"Export as PowerPoint"** (the download icon)
2. A pop-up window titled **"PowerPoint Export with AI Review"** appears

### Step 2: Generate Slide Previews

You have two options:

- **"Generate Previews"** â€” Creates preview images of your slides so you can review them before exporting (recommended)
- **"Skip Review & Export"** â€” Downloads the PowerPoint file immediately without previewing

If you chose **"Generate Previews"**, wait while the system creates slide images. You'll see: *"Generating slide previews..."*

### Step 3: Preview Your Slides

Once previews are ready, you'll see a grid of slide thumbnails. Each slide shows:
- A preview image
- A label indicating its type (Title, Contents, Divider, Chart, Insights, or Timeline)
- A slide number

You can click any slide to select and highlight it.

### Step 4: Get AI Design Review (optional but recommended)

Click **"Get AI Review"** to have the system check your slides for design issues.

You'll see: *"Analyzing slides with AI..."*

### Step 5: Review Suggestions

After the review, you'll see:

**Design Score** â€” A score out of 100 indicating overall design quality

**Suggestions** â€” A list of recommendations grouped by importance:

- **High Priority** (red) â€” Issues that should be fixed before distribution
  - Examples: Title too long (over 80 characters), too many bullet points (6+), placeholder content detected, wrong background colour
- **Medium Priority** (yellow) â€” Issues worth reviewing
  - Examples: Generic chart title, subtitle too long, source format inconsistent
- **Low Priority** (green) â€” Minor improvements
  - Examples: Spacing tweaks, font size variations, image resolution

**Strengths** â€” Things the system found that look good

Each suggestion shows:
- What the issue is
- Which slide it affects (you can click the slide number to see it)
- The current value and what it should be
- A checkbox to select it for future reference

High priority suggestions are automatically selected.

### Step 6: Export

Click **"Export PowerPoint"** to download the file.

The file will be named after your report title (e.g., *"Global Market Outlook â€” January 2026.pptx"*).

> **Tip:** You don't have to fix suggestions before exporting. The review is informational â€” it shows you what could be improved, but the export always generates the full presentation.

## 20. Tips and Best Practices

### Preparing Data for Charts

- **Clean your data first** â€” Remove empty rows and columns before uploading
- **Use clear column headers** â€” The first row should contain descriptive names (e.g., "Year", "Revenue", "Growth Rate")
- **First column = categories** â€” The leftmost column should contain your categories, dates, or labels
- **Keep it focused** â€” 3â€“5 data series work best for readability
- **Use consistent formats** â€” Don't mix percentages and whole numbers in the same column

### Uploading Chart Images

- **Use high-resolution images** â€” The clearer the image, the more accurate the data extraction
- **Include visible labels** â€” Make sure axis labels and data labels are readable
- **Use simple charts** â€” Complex or heavily decorated charts may not extract cleanly
- **Check the extracted data** â€” Always review the data table after uploading an image and correct any values that were misread

### Writing Effective Content

- **Use bullet points** rather than long paragraphs â€” they display better in both web and PowerPoint
- **Keep titles concise** â€” Under 80 characters for the best PowerPoint output
- **Keep subtitles supporting** â€” Under 200 characters
