Tariffs: de-escalating without any retaliation

Making America stronger again?

Source: Source A

The US administration is focused on tariff revenues to fund promised tax cuts.

Deals cover more than 60% of the “reciprocal tariffs” and the top 6 trading partners.

After consumption shifts, the average tariff rate will be 17.3%, the highest since 1935.

The latest monthly net receipts reached $26bn in June. All else being equal, this is $350bn of revenue p.a. (1.2% of US GDP).

Earnings season

Dominance of Mag 7 to be followed by broader growth participation in 2026

Source: Source A

For Q2 2025 (with 34% of S&P 500 companies reporting actual results), 80% of S&P 500 companies have reported a positive earnings per share (EPS) surprise.

For Q3 2025, analysts are projecting earnings growth of 7.6% and 7% for Q4 2025, leading to 9.6% earnings growth for 2025 and 13.9% for 2026.

The Mag 7 are expected to report growth of 14.1% for Q2, ahead of the 3.4% expected for the others.

Looking ahead, the “S&P 493” earnings growth is expected to accelerate.
